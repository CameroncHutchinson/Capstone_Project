Scenario

You are a junior data analyst working in the marketing analyst team at Cyclistic, a bike-share company in Chicago. The director of marketing believes the company’s future success depends on maximizing the number of annual memberships. Therefore, your team wants to understand how casual riders and annual members use Cyclistic bikes differently. From these insights, your team will design a new marketing strategy to convert casual riders into annual members. But first, Cyclistic executives must approve your recommendations, so they must be backed up with compelling data insights and professional data visualizations.

About the Company

In 2016, Cyclistic launched a successful bike-share offering. Since then, the program has grown to a fleet of 5,824 bicycles that are geotracked and locked into a network of 692 stations across Chicago. The bikes can be unlocked from one station and returned to any other station in the system anytime. Until now, Cyclistic’s marketing strategy relied on building general awareness and appealing to broad consumer segments. One approach that helped make these things possible was the flexibility of its pricing plans: single-ride passes, full-day passes, and annual memberships. Customers who purchase single-ride or full-day passes are referred to as casual riders. Customers who purchase annual memberships are Cyclistic members.

Business Task

Derive insights on the differences between casual riders and member rides through data analysis.Design marketing strategies aimed at converting casual riders into annual members.

Ask

We are required to answer the following questions:

1. What is the problem you are trying to solve?

We are trying to differentiate casual and member rider usage to increase memberships.

2. How can your insights drive business decisions?

By analyzing the behaviors and differences between the two groups, we can derive recommendations and an effective marketing campaign for our customers.

bike_df_2 = bike_df_2 %>% rename(member_type = member_casual)

bike_df_2$day_of_week <- wday(bike_df_2$started_at, label = T, abbr = T)

bike_df_2$month <- format(as.Date(bike_df_2$started_at), '%m')

#Adding trip Duration Column using difftime()
bike_df_2$trip_duration <- difftime(bike_df_2$ended_at, bike_df_2$started_at, units = 'mins')

#convert trip_duration to numeric to run calculations
bike_df_2$trip_duration <- as.numeric(as.character(bike_df_2$trip_duration))

#Removing bad data where ride length is <= 0
bike_df_2<- bike_df_2[!(bike_df_2$trip_duration <=0),]

We can now answer the following questions after analyzing the data:

1. How should you organize your data to perform analysis on it?

I formatted the Trip_Duration column from character to numeric values using the as. Numeric function. All previous columns were formatted correctly. Moreover, I added new columns, such as day_of_week, start_hour, and Month. I renamed the member_casual column to member_type for clarification. Lastly, I removed unneeded columns, such as start and end stations, and the longitude and. latitude of bikes.

2. Has your data been appropriately formatted?

The data is formatted correctly. I used the str() function to confirm the data types of each column. I converted the Trip_Duration column from a character to a numeric type because the data is numbers. I also formatted the Trip_Duration column to return the values by minutes as the unit.

3. What surprises did you discover in the data?

  • Classic bikes were more popular than electric bikes for both member types. this is surprising as I thought people would prefer electric-assisted bikes.
  • Member riders did not use docked bikes. I assume members prefer docked bikes because they are more familiar with the company systems. I’d imagine members would gear toward secured bikes after consistently using the bikes.
  • Casual rider’s trip duration was longer than members’ trip duration.

4. What trends or relationships did you find in the data?

  • Members used classic and electric bikes more than casual riders
  • Members rarely used docked bikes
  • The average ride was 21.11 minutes.
  • The most popular day to ride for casuals and members is Saturday
  • Member usage decreases as you move from weekday to weekend
  • Member and casual riders used bikes most frequently during the summer.
  • Members used bikes more frequently than casuals during the winter season.
  • Casual riders used bikes more frequently than members during the summer season.
  • The top 10 Casual rider’s start stations were near tourist attractions
  • Members constitute a much larger population in the dataset than casuals

5. Did you answer how casual and member rides use bicycles differently?

Casuals: Use bikes more frequently during the summer, weekends, and leisure time. Casual riders use regular and electric bikes more frequently than docked bikes. Casual riders ride near tourist areas for enjoyment. Average trip duration was longer than to member rides.

Members: Use bikes more frequently during the summer, throughout the week, and for transportation purposes. Members ride regular and electric bikes more frequently than docked bikes.
